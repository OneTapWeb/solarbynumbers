The scaffolding went up on a Monday; by Wednesday evening the roof was generating.

The quote was for **21 × Aiko 475W** panels — 9.98 kWp across our east/west roof. On the day,
the installers measured up and found room for one more on the west face. No drama, no extra
charge: **22 panels, 10.45 kWp**. It pays to have an installer who treats the roof as the spec
rather than the paperwork.

The rest of the kit:

- **Sigenergy EC 8.0SP** hybrid inverter — 8 kW, with the EMS built in
- **SigenStor BAT 6.0 + BAT 10.0** stacked — 15.06 kWh nominal, 14.6 usable
- G99 approval came back from the DNO with **no export limit**, which matters more than it
  sounds: the full 8 kW can flow out during VPP events and Saving Sessions

First impressions of the Sigenergy kit: the stack design is genuinely tidy (the battery modules
and inverter click together like oversized LEGO), the app is fine, and — the bit we really care
about — it speaks **Modbus TCP over the LAN**, which is what makes everything in
[the stack](/stack/) possible.

What the sales projection says this system should do: ~7,500 kWh/yr generation and roughly
£1,900–2,000 of year-1 value against an £11,999 price. Whether that survives contact with
reality is the whole point of [the stats page](/stats/).
